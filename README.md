## üéì Leadership Philosophy

My approach to leadership centers on **psychological safety, strategic pragmatism, and low-ego coaching**:

### Core Principles

1. **Protect Team Culture Over Perfect Outcomes**
   - Sometimes imperfect solutions that preserve team wellbeing beat theoretically optimal approaches
   - Transparent about constraints: "I can't fire him, but here's how I'll protect you"
   - Psychological safety correlates with product quality (especially in high-stakes AI like healthcare)

2. **Influence > Authority**
   - Build alliances, create paper trails, leverage ego strategically
   - Navigate matrix organizations without formal power through collaboration and credibility
   - Meet teams where they are; offer help, not just asks

3. **Empower Domain Experts**
   - Self-service validation tools > gatekeeping technical decisions
   - Give non-technical stakeholders deployment control with appropriate guardrails
   - Democratizing control accelerates innovation

4. **Strategic Knowledge Management**
   - Never confront toxic high-performers until you've eliminated their "irreplaceable" status
   - Document institutional knowledge disguised as team expansion
   - Succession planning is insurance policy

5. **Pragmatic Problem-Solving**
   - Accept 80% solutions that are politically possible over 100% solutions that aren't
   - Honest transparency builds more trust than false promises
   - Sometimes you must pause feature delivery to fix foundations (infrastructure before scale)
